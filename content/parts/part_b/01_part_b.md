# Part B — Influenza Surveillance



## Scenario



In both 2021 and 2023, the country's economy was hit hard by outbreaks of avian influenza on its poultry farms, resulting in large-scale chicken mortality and reduced poultry exports. The government provided funding to the Ministry of Health's Influenza Program to strengthen human and poultry influenza surveillance, including improved molecular testing.



Since free health care is provided to all residents of Country Z, the government was confident that it could identify most influenza cases in the country and monitor for influenza viruses with pandemic potential. The following influenza surveillance activities are currently being conducted.



### Surveillance of human influenza infections (includes all types)



- Case-based reporting by healthcare facilities, with collection of clinical specimens suitable for traditional laboratory testing from a subset of suspected cases.

- Weekly reporting of the number of cases of influenza-like illness from health facilities, including "0" reporting when no cases are identified.



Minimum data collection requirements include clinical, demographic and laboratory specimen collection data.



To strengthen surveillance for human infections with avian influenza and novel influenza A viruses, the Influenza Program in Country Z added the below activities.



### Enhanced Influenza Surveillance



- Laboratory report of all positive influenza results, including "0" report when there are no positive results.

- Improved laboratory molecular testing in a subset of suspect cases to identify novel avian influenza viruses.

- Monthly visits to health centers and laboratories by district public health personnel to identify any unreported cases.
