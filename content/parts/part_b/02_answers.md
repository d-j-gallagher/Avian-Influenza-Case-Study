# Part B — Instructor Guidance

## Answer 13 (key points)
- **Both**
  - **Active surveillance:** public health officers collect data from health facilities and make routine visits and phone calls.
  - **Passive surveillance:** notifications from health centers and laboratories to district public health offices.

## Answer 14 (key points)
- To distinguish between **no cases** and **no notification** of cases.
- Zero-reporting ensures officials know facilities/labs truly did not diagnose cases, rather than underreporting or not reporting.
- This distinction is especially important during disease elimination efforts.
