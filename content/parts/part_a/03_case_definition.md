# Case definition for avian influenza infection in humans

> Note: This content was extracted from the Instructor Guide table. If you prefer perfect formatting, you can keep this as an image instead.

## Probable case
Recent exposure (<10 days) to avian influenza A virus through one of the following:
- Exposure to infected birds
- Exposure to an infected person
- Laboratory exposure

**AND** clinical criteria including one or more of the following:
- Signs/symptoms consistent with acute or lower respiratory tract infection (e.g., cough, sore throat, fever, runny nose, fatigue, myalgia, arthralgia, headache, etc.)
- Conjunctivitis
- Complications of acute respiratory diseases without identified cause

A person meeting the criterion for avian influenza A virus infection above and for whom laboratory test results do not provide a sufficient level of detail to confirm HPAI virus infection.

## Suspect case
Meets the definition of probable case **AND** the results of confirmatory laboratory tests are unknown or pending.

## Confirmed case
Meets the definition of probable case **AND** a designated laboratory using approved methodologies has confirmed infection by a positive test.
