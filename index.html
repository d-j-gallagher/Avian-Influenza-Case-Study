Learning Objectives

After completing this case study, you should be able to:

  • List and describe the steps of the surveillance cycle
  • Describe the purpose and use of local surveillance data
  • Compare human and avian surveillance data
  • Complete a surveillance case report form
  • Calculate and interpret incidence rates and case-fatality rates
  • Identify and correct data errors
  • Summarize and interpret surveillance data by person, place, and time
  • Identify how One Health can be applied to strengthening surveillance systems

Part A: Review of Influenza Data

The year is 2024. On a Monday morning in early March, you started a new job as a surveillance officer focusing on influenza at the District D public health office, located in Province A in Country Z. Before you arrived, you reviewed your notes on surveillance.

An estimated 85,000 people live in District D, where you are assigned. The district has one hospital (the General Hospital) and three health clinics: the District Clinic, the Children's Clinic, and the General Clinic.
