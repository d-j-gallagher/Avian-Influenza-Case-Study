Part B: Influenza Surveillance

In both 2021 and 2023, the country's economy was hit hard by outbreaks of avian influenza on its poultry farms, resulting in large-scale chicken mortality and reduced poultry exports. The government provided funding to the Ministry of Health's Influenza Program to strengthen human and poultry influenza surveillance, including improved molecular testing.

Current Influenza Surveillance Activities:
  • Case-based reporting by healthcare facilities
  • Weekly reporting of influenza-like illness cases
  • Laboratory report of all positive influenza results
  • Improved laboratory molecular testing for novel avian influenza viruses
  • Monthly visits to health centers and laboratories by district public health personnel

Part F: One Health Investigation

Due to the avian influenza outbreaks in 2021 and 2023 and their associated economic losses, the Ministry of Agriculture decided to vaccinate domestic chickens and ducks to control and potentially prevent future avian influenza outbreaks.

You meet with the veterinary surveillance officer to review studies about highly pathogenic avian influenza (H5N1) vaccines and immune responses in poultry.

Study Background: A study of H5N1 in chickens and ducks raised on small farms sampled birds monthly from May 2021 to May 2022. Chickens are raised entirely indoors, while ducks are allowed outdoors during the day. The overall H5 antibody prevalence during the study period was 43% for ducks and 19% for chickens in unvaccinated birds.

Question 28: Why might there be a difference between the seroprevalence of ducks and chickens?

Question 29: The study showed peaks in antibody prevalence in January and May. What could be the cause of these peaks?

Hint: In this area of Vietnam, rice harvesting takes place in January and May. During this time, farmers often allow ducks to wander in their fields.

Question 30: Vaccination campaigns were conducted in September and April. After vaccination, the antibody seroprevalence was 54% for ducks and 56% for chickens.

a) Was antibody seroprevalence higher in vaccinated compared to unvaccinated chickens and ducks?

b) Should vaccination be recommended for the flocks in the study?

c) Are the vaccination campaigns being conducted at an appropriate time?

Part G: Results Report

With your analysis report in hand, you and your supervisor sat down for an informal meeting with the District Chief Medical Officer (DCMO).

The DCMO almost immediately asked you if you thought the human influenza incidence rate would be lower in 2024 than in 2023 (64 cases/100,000).

Key Facts:
  • District D population: 85,000
  • As of November 2024: 44 cases reported
  • 2023 incidence rate: 64 per 100,000
  • Fewer influenza cases typically occur during winter months (November to February) in Country Z
